History of Rexburg:

Founded in 1883 by Thomas Edwin Ricks, Rexburg, Idaho, has a vibrant history. Rexburg was founded by settlers who set up on the banks of the Teton River. These settlers were LDS Church members who, in 1883, were instructed by Thomas E Ricks to settle the valley. Rexburg is also honored by the USS Rexburg, a Navy research ship named after the city. Just 5 years after settling, the locals founded Ricks Academy now BYU-Idaho. BYU-Idaho has over 19,000 students attending each semester.

Explore Rexburg:

Nestled at an elevation of 4,865 feet, Rexburg offers a unique geographical experience. The city sits atop a shield volcano, providing a picturesque backdrop. With its close proximity to the St. Anthony Sand Dunes, Yellowstone National Park's West Entrance, and the stunning Teton Range, Rexburg is an ideal base for exploring diverse natural wonders and recreational opportunities. The area's volcanic history has left a lasting impact, enriching the soil and making Rexburg renowned for its great Idaho potatoes.
